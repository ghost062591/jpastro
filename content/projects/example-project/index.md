The Andromeda Galaxy (M31) is our nearest large galactic neighbor at approximately 2.537 million light-years away. This was captured over two nights under excellent transparency from a dark site in central Texas.

The galaxy's dust lanes and satellite galaxies M32 and M110 are visible in the final image.
